Spaghetti Carbonara

This easy pasta sauce will cook in about the same time it takes to cook some dried pasta in boiling salted water-we're talking about quick and easy options here. These amounts serve 2 but are easy to scale up or down. Remember you want about 75g of dried pasta per person-use whatever shape you've got. Or, check out my super simple method for fresh pasta, using just flour and water.

    Ingredients

  • 150g dried spaghetti
  • 2 rashers of high-welfare smoked streaky bacon
  • 1/2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 free-range eggs
  • 30g Parmesan cheese, plus extra for grating

    Steps

  1. Cook the pasta in a pan of boiling salted water according to packet instructions
  2. Slice the bacon and place in a non-stick pan on a medium heat with half a tablespoon of olive oil and a really good pinch of black pepper. Leave it to get super-golden and crispy, tossing ocassionally, the turn off the heat.
  3. Meanwhile, beat the eggs in a bowl, and finely grate in the Parmesan and mix well
  4. Use ngs to transfer your pasta straight into the pan and toss
  5. Pour the Parmesan eggs into a pan, and keep everything moving, loosening with splashes of the pasta cooking water until you have a silky sauce. Make sure the pan is not too hot otherwise the eggs will scramble.
  6. Plate up the pasta, and finish with an extra grating of Parmesan
